Used to classify diseases and other health problems recorded on many types of health and vital records including death certificates and health records. In addition to enabling the storage and retrieval of diagnostic information for clinical, epidemiological and quality purposes, these records also provide the basis for the compilation of national mortality and morbidity statistics by WHO Member States. ICD-10 was endorsed by the Forty-third World Health Assembly in May 1990 and has been in use in WHO Member States since 1994.

Source Home Page: http://www.who.int/classifications/icd/en/

The World Health Organization allows use of ICD-10 in NCI Enterprise Vocabulary Services, but requires licensing for other purposes (see http://www.who.int/classifications/icd/en/).
